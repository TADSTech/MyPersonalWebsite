Devlog #4

The Terminal, The Phone, and Everything In Between

Full terminal overlay, mobile optimization, shake-to-open. The corners go touch-friendly.

The site had a solid core — hero, about, stats, hidden corners. But it was desktop-only. The corners relied on hover. On a phone you'd see nothing.

Not anymore.

Mobile optimization

Layout stacks at 768px and 480px. Grids collapse to single column. Fonts scale down. Nothing breaks.

The corners go touch-friendly

Hover doesn't exist on a phone. So corner zones now respond to taps. Tap a corner → panel slides open. Tap outside → it closes. Auto-hides after 4 seconds.

The full terminal experience

Press Ctrl + ` anywhere and a full-screen terminal slides down. Title bar with traffic-light dots. Green-on-black output. Blinking prompt. Command history with arrow keys. Tab autocomplete. 25+ commands including whoami, neofetch, matrix, cowsay, ping, theme, and more.

Shake to open (mobile)

On a phone, shake it and the terminal opens. Uses the DeviceMotion API. A deliberate shake triggers it, a normal bump won't.

Everything ships as static files. No framework. No build step. Just HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
